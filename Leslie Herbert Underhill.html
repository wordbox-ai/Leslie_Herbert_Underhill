Dr. Leslie Herbert Underhill

(7 January 1929 – 15 February 2003)

Early Life and Education

Leslie Herbert Underhill was born in 1929 in Ilford, Essex, England. His family relocated to Wales during his childhood, where he attended Aberdare Boys' Grammar School in Glamorgan. Demonstrating exceptional academic talent from an early age, in the 1944 Central Welsh Board (CWB) School Certificate examinations, he achieved strong results across multiple subjects, laying the foundation for his scholarly pursuits.

By 1947, he excelled in the CWB Higher Certificate examinations, earning distinctions in pure mathematics, applied mathematics, and physics. These results secured him a prestigious State Scholarship and one of the top Price-Davies Scholarships to the University College of Wales (UCW), Aberystwyth. The headmaster's report from the school's 1948 certificate ceremony highlighted his achievement as one of the highest open awards available for entry into the university.

During his university years (c. 1947–1951), from which he graduated with a double first-class honours degree in mathematics and physics, he developed a keen interest in photography. A collection of his photographs survives, providing a vivid snapshot of post-war student life in Wales. Following his undergraduate success, Underhill pursued postgraduate studies at University College London (UCL), earning a PhD in theoretical physics. His doctoral research focused on nuclear interactions, particularly the scattering of nucleons by alpha particles—a key area in mid-20th-century nuclear physics.

Professional Career

Dr. Underhill's career was deeply intertwined with Britain's post-war nuclear research efforts, much of which was classified. Upon completing his PhD in the mid-1950s, he joined the United Kingdom Atomic Energy Authority (UKAEA). He began his tenure at the Atomic Weapons Research Establishment (AWRE) at Aldermaston, Berkshire, rising to the position of Senior Scientific Officer, where he applied his expertise to weapons-related modeling.

In 1957, at the age of 28, Underhill received a European Council Fellowship to conduct advanced research at the European Organization for Nuclear Research (CERN) in Geneva. This prestigious award underscored his emerging prominence, allowing him to collaborate on high-energy particle experiments during CERN's formative years.

He later transferred to UKAEA's Winfrith site, a hub for reactor development, before settling at the Risley headquarters. By the 1990s, his role had evolved with the field's shift toward computational methods; he served as head of central computer services, managing data processing for nuclear simulations and safety analyses until his retirement. Notably, in 1993–1994, he contributed to the OECD Nuclear Energy Agency's Computer Program Service (CPS), coordinating computational resources across UKAEA sites.

Due to the classified nature of much of his later UKAEA work, detailed records are sparse, but his trajectory—from theorist to computational leader—mirrors the evolution of nuclear research from manual calculations to digital simulations.

Key Publications and Reports

  • The Scattering of Nucleons by Alpha Particles – the s-Phases (1954)

    Co-authored with S. Hochberg & H.S.W. Massey. Analyzed phase shifts in neutron and proton scattering to model low-energy interactions.

  • The Scattering of Nucleons by Alpha Particles – Determination of the Spin-Orbit Interaction (1955)

    Co-authored with S. Hochberg, H.S.W. Massey, & H. Robertson. Quantified spin-orbit forces in two-body nuclear systems, influencing later shell model theories.

  • The growth of complexity of a general-purpose program (1963)

    Published in The Computer Journal.

Personal Life, Retirement, and Legacy

Underhill married Olwen Underhill, and the couple resided in Gayhurst Avenue, Fearnhead, Warrington, for 37 years. They had one son, one daughter, two grandsons, and one granddaughter. An avid outdoorsman, he enjoyed walking in the Welsh hills, photography, and tennis. He was a long-standing committee member of the Stockton Heath Tennis Club and served as treasurer for the Warrington Tennis League and Warrington Sports Council.

In retirement, he remained active in alumni networks, joining the Aberystwyth Old Students' Association and the Appleton Probus Club. Leslie Herbert Underhill passed away on February 15, 2003, at the age of 74. Following a private service in Mid Wales, a thanksgiving service was held on March 10, 2003, at Bethesda Evangelical Church in Warrington.

His legacy endures through his scientific papers, which continue to inform nuclear scattering models, and his photographic archive, offering a personal glimpse into mid-century Welsh academia. His State Scholarship and CERN fellowship highlight his role in shaping Britain's scientific talent pipeline.
